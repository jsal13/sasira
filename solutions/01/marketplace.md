# Marketplace Discoveries - Solutions

## Context
Discovered on small clay tablets among the ruins of what appears to be merchant stalls in the old marketplace. The tablets were remarkably well preserved, suggesting they were fired clay rather than simple dried mud. Several tablets were found together, indicating they may have been part of a merchant's records or public announcements.

## Text Translations

### Text 1 - Merchant's Clay Tablet
**Sasiran:** სა აش სირ ნარს  
**Translation:** "I see water nine"  
**Analysis:** Simple merchant record, possibly noting inventory of water containers (9 units)

### Text 2 - Market Announcement Board  
**Sasiran:** შალიმ სომ ას ის  
**Translation:** "People come one to"  
**Analysis:** Public announcement, possibly about gathering or single-file entry

### Text 3 - Trader's Personal Tablet
**Sasiran:** მოს ლის ორ ზურ  
**Translation:** "Child friend and love"  
**Analysis:** Personal inscription, possibly about family or emotional connection

### Text 4 - Price or Quantity Marker
**Sasiran:** შას შის უს  
**Translation:** "Six seven three"  
**Analysis:** Numerical sequence, likely quantities or prices for market goods

## Grammar Notes
- Simple present tense constructions
- Basic SOV word order maintained
- Numerical sequences for merchant record-keeping
- Personal pronouns and basic nouns predominant
