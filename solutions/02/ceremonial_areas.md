# Ceremonial Areas - Solutions

## Context
Inscribed on bronze plaques that were attached to what appears to be ceremonial altars throughout the temple complex. The plaques show signs of regular polishing and care, suggesting they were important to the inhabitants and actively maintained during regular ceremonies and rituals.

## Text Translations

### Text 1 - Main Ceremonial Altar Plaque
**Sasiran:** ზალ ხაშუ აზ შულ სომ ულ ლასუ შინ ალ ზურ იშუ ხის სირალ  
**Translation:** "Dark magic from elder comes but life light gives, strong spirit will live"  
**Analysis:** Complex ceremonial text about overcoming darkness through spiritual light and strength

### Text 2 - Secondary Altar Inscription  
**Sasiran:** აშუ შარ შალიმ ურ იშუ სურ ან სარ  
**Translation:** "God wind people upon, new spirits within are"  
**Analysis:** Standard blessing about divine presence filling people with new spiritual energy

### Text 3 - Ritual Preparation Area
**Sasiran:** ნურ შალ ზურ ორ მირ სარ  
**Translation:** "Ancient person strong and fragile is"  
**Analysis:** Meditation on human nature - recognition of both strength and vulnerability

### Text 4 - Offering Bowl Rim
**Sasiran:** ზურუ შალ საი აშუश შარუ სარ  
**Translation:** "Prophecy pure, this god's blessing is"  
**Analysis:** Consecration text identifying the bowl as vessel for divine prophecy

## Grammar Notes
- Complex sentence structures with conjunctions (ორ, ულ)
- Future tense constructions (სირალ)
- Prepositions for spatial relationships (ურ, ან, აზ)
- Religious and ceremonial vocabulary
- Past and present tense mixing in ritual contexts
